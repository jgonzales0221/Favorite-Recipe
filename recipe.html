Korean Beef Bulgogi

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds boneless ribeye steak
  • 1/2 small pear, peeled and coarsely grated
  • 1/4 cup reduced sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons toasted sesame oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon freshly grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds

Additional Ingredients

  • 2 cups of white rice and 4 cups of water
  • Romaine lettuce
  • Pickled ginger

Notes * If a cast iron grill pan is unavailable, a large cast iron skillet can serve as an alternative.

Instructions

Before beginning the recipe, wrap the pound and a half of boneless ribeye steak in a bag and put it in the freezer for 30 minutes. After cooling, slice the ribeye steak across the grain into Â¼-inch thick pieces and place them in a Ziploc bag.


Put the mixture of pear, soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and gochujang in with the sliced meat and marinate for at least 2 hours to overnight, making sure to occasionally turn the bag. Once marinate add 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a cast iron grill pan over medium-high heat.


Cook the steak in batches, laying it out in a single layer in the pan. Flip it once to ensure it is charred and fully cooked, which should take about 2-3 minutes per side. Repeat this process with the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil and steak.


In parallel, prepare 2 cups of white rice by cooking it in 4 cups of cold water. Bring it to a boil, then cover and reduce the heat to simmer for 20 minutes.


Prepare romaine lettuce by cutting off the bottom, splitting the leaves, and rinsing them well. Finely chop the green onions for garnish and sprinkle sesame seeds on top.


Serve the dish with a base of rice, bulgogi on top, or use a romaine lettuce leaf as a taco, layering rice and bulgogi inside. For an extra burst of flavor, add pickled ginger if desired.
